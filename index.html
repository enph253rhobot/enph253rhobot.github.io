About the Competition

Every year, the UBC Engineering Physics department runs a rapid prototyping course for students finishing their second year. Students are divided into teams of four, and spend about 6 weeks designing and building a robot to accomplish some task. At the end of the course, a competition is held between teams in the class. For the summer of 2016, the competition surface was a miniature 8 x 16 foot city, with roads as black electrical tape, and stuffed animal passengers distributed around the city. The goal of the competition was to navigate the city and collect passengers along the way. Once a passenger is collected, the robot was to bring it to the destination for drop-off. One of the main challenges of the competition was to be racing against an opposing robot to collect the most passengers within a 2 minute time frame. See Figure 1 for a visualization. The dotted lines in Figure 1 denote city blocks, the red lines denote black electrical tape (roads), and solid blue lines denote competition surface boundaries.
